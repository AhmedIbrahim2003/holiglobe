History of Chinese New Year

Chinese New Year, also known as the Spring Festival, has a storied history dating back over 3,000 years. Its origins trace to the Shang Dynasty (c. 1600–1046 BCE) when people celebrated the lunar new year as an agricultural festival. Over time, it evolved into a more formalized event during the Han Dynasty (202 BCE–220 CE), with activities like ancestor worship and the use of firecrackers to ward off evil spirits. The adoption of the lunar calendar by Emperor Wu of the Han Dynasty solidified the festival's connection to the cycles of the moon. While its traditions have evolved, Chinese New Year remains deeply rooted in ancient customs, continuing to be one of the most significant and widely celebrated festivals in Chinese culture.
